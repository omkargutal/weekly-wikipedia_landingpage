Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi
(2 October 1869-30 January 1948)
Indian Freedom Fighter


Mahatma Gandhi, whose name needs no introduction, is revered as the Father of the Indian Independence Movement. Born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, he went on to become a global icon of nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience.
Gandhi's early life was marked by modesty and simplicity. He studied law in England, but his heart was always in India, where he would eventually return to spearhead a movement that would change the course of history.
Gandhi's simplicity was evident in his personal life. He dressed in a loincloth and shawl, lived in a communal ashram, and adhered to a vegetarian diet. These choices were not just personal but political. They were a statement against the excesses of materialism and a call for Indians to embrace self-reliance and self-discipline.
Tragically, Gandhi's life was cut short on January 30, 1948, when he was assassinated by a fanatic in New Delhi. However, his teachings and ideals continue to resonate with people of all backgrounds and beliefs. His life is a testament to the power of one individual's unwavering commitment to truth, justice, and nonviolence in the pursuit of a better world. Mahatma Gandhi will forever be remembered as a beacon of hope and a symbol of the enduring human spirit's ability to bring about change through peaceful means
